## Executive Summary

This document reviews publicly available case studies from Alianza's website showcasing successful implementations of their cloud communications platform across various service providers. The case studies demonstrate Alianza's effectiveness in solving critical business challenges for ISPs, cable operators, fiber providers, and telecommunications companies.

**Key Findings:**
- 5+ major case studies identified covering diverse industry segments
- Common themes: cost reduction, operational efficiency, network monetization, and improved customer experience
- All case studies demonstrate measurable ROI and business transformation
- Strong focus on rural and regional service providers

## Case Study 1: Horizon Telcom

**Company Profile:**
- **Industry**: Telecommunications
- **History**: Over 125 years in operation
- **Location**: Ohio and neighboring states
- **Network**: ~5,000 route miles of fiber optic infrastructure
- **Customer Segments**: Healthcare, education, government, wireless carriers, data centers, enterprise

### Challenges

Horizon faced significant limitations with their existing VoIP partner:
- Limited customer features and functionality
- Inability to service their entire footprint
- Rising consumer demands for collaboration and communication solutions
- Decision point: Build their own solution or find a comprehensive partner

### Alianza Solution

**Products Deployed:**
- **Business Cloud Communications (BCC)**: Full unified communications suite
- **SIP Trunking**: Replaced existing Metaswitch softswitch
- **Horizon Hosted Voice**: New cloud-based branded offering

### Results & Benefits

1. **Revenue Growth**
   - Created multiple tiered customer offerings
   - New highly lucrative revenue streams
   - Ability to serve all customer segments with true anywhere connectivity
   - Service expansion beyond their physical footprint

2. **Network Monetization**
   - Clear business case for monetizing existing fiber network
   - Over-the-top (OTT) voice and data solutions

3. **Cost Reduction**
   - Eliminated switch infrastructure OPEX
   - Reduced engineering, administration, and support personnel costs
   - Eliminated software maintenance costs

4. **Scalability**
   - Seats scale up and down to meet customer needs
   - Monthly costs aligned with growth and margins

## Case Study 3: Service Electric Cable TV & Communications

**Company Profile:**
- **Industry**: Cable Television & Communications
- **History**: Nation's first cable TV company (founded 1948)
- **Location**: New Jersey and Pennsylvania
- **Services**: Cable TV, broadband Internet, telephone (via PenTeleData partnership)

### Challenges

**Critical Business Bottlenecks:**
- Existing voice provider maintained strict platform control
- SECTV was "never allowed to make an order" independently
- Substantial delays for customers during line activation and porting
- Third-party bottlenecks severely impacted customer onboarding
- High operational costs with limited control

### Alianza Solution

**Implementation:**
- Cloud VoIP platform upgrade (2017)
- Direct service delivery from Alianza data center in Ashburn, Virginia
- Network architecture: Services delivered over SECTV's private cable network
- SECTV controls bandwidth and quality of service

### Results & Benefits

1. **Operational Agility**
   - Quote: "Alianza's product and platform agility enabled SECTV to install the customer the same morning they received an order"
   - Eliminated third-party approval bottlenecks
   - Full control over customer activation and provisioning

2. **Cost Reduction**
   - Substantially reduced operational costs
   - Streamlined operations

3. **Service Quality**
   - Direct control over bandwidth and QoS
   - Services remain on private network for optimal performance

4. **Customer Experience**
   - Same-day installation capability
   - Faster activation and porting processes

**Sources:**
- [Success Story - Service Electric Cable TV | Alianza](https://www.alianza.com/success-stories/service-electric-cable-tv/)

## Case Study 4: Elevate Fiber (Delta-Montrose Electric Association)

**Company Profile:**
- **Industry**: Electric Cooperative & Fiber Broadband Provider
- **Parent**: Delta-Montrose Electric Association (DMEA)
- **Scale**: 36,000+ electric meters, 12,600+ broadband subscribers
- **Location**: Delta and Montrose counties, Colorado
- **Mission**: Quality broadband to every community within their electric service footprint

### Background & Challenges

**Origin Story:**
- Membership requested internet services in 2015 for "quality broadband at an affordable price"
- Electric co-op expanded into telecommunications

**Technical & Financial Challenges:**
- Initial Metaswitch Class 5 softswitch proved too expensive
- Tandem trunking and direct-end-office trunking were "very expensive and unruly to properly size and maintain"
- Major cost burden: $35,000/year maintenance and support for only 2,000 subscribers
- Softswitch licensing, PSTN connectivity, and maintenance costs "not easily absorbed within a respectable retail rate"
- Unsustainable cost structure threatened business viability

### Alianza Solution

**Selection Process:**
- Evaluated regional service providers and nationwide vendors
- Alianza's extensive migration experience was deciding factor

**Products Deployed:**
- Cloud voice and trunking solution
- Cloud PBX for small businesses (analog phones and key systems)

### Results & Benefits

1. **Financial Predictability**
   - Quote: "In the 3 years [with Alianza] we've not overrun our budget one time"
   - Eliminated cost overruns and budget surprises
   - Sustainable pricing model

2. **Market Success - High Take Rate**
   - Quote: "The quality of the [Alianza] service, with the uptime reliability and the call quality that consumers expect, has allowed us to sustain a 16% take rate"
   - 16% take rate is "fairly unique in the telecom industry as cord-cutting has become so wide-spread"
   - Strong customer retention despite industry headwinds

3. **Business Market Penetration**
   - Quote: Alianza's Cloud PBX "has been key for us being able to maintain a place in our market"
   - Simple solution for small businesses
   - Competitive positioning against larger providers

4. **Service Quality**
   - High uptime reliability
   - Call quality meets consumer expectations
   - Supports competitive differentiation

## Cross-Case Analysis

### Common Themes

#### 1. **Customer Profile**
- Predominantly regional and rural service providers
- Mix of cable operators, fiber providers, fixed wireless ISPs, and electric co-ops
- Companies seeking to add or upgrade voice/communications services
- Focus on underserved markets

#### 2. **Pain Points Addressed**
- **Cost**: Unsustainable legacy infrastructure costs (Elevate Fiber: $35K/year for 2,000 subs)
- **Control**: Third-party bottlenecks limiting operational agility (Service Electric)
- **Features**: Limited functionality and inability to compete (Horizon)
- **Scale**: Inability to serve entire footprint or scale efficiently
- **Quality**: Poor service quality and reliability issues (Adams Cable)

#### 3. **Solution Components**
- Cloud-native, software-as-a-service (SaaS) platform
- Business Cloud Communications (UCaaS)
- SIP Trunking
- Cloud PBX for SMB customers
- White-label/branded solutions (Cymbus, Horizon Hosted Voice)

#### 4. **Business Outcomes**

| Outcome Category | Evidence |
|-----------------|----------|
| **Revenue Growth** | New tiered offerings, service expansion beyond footprint, 16% take rate |
| **Cost Reduction** | Eliminated OPEX, reduced personnel costs, budget predictability |
| **Operational Efficiency** | Same-day installation, elimination of bottlenecks, scalable seats |
| **Network Monetization** | OTT services, fiber network monetization |
| **Market Competitiveness** | Feature parity, SMB solutions, quality differentiation |

#### 5. **Implementation Success Factors**
- Carrier-grade reliability and security
- Flexibility and scalability
- Migration expertise and support
- Platform agility for rapid deployment
- White-label capabilities

### Industry Insights

#### **Market Positioning**
Alianza focuses on enabling regional service providers to compete with national carriers by:
- Providing enterprise-grade features previously unavailable
- Offering cost-effective cloud alternatives to expensive legacy infrastructure
- Enabling rapid deployment and operational agility
- Supporting multi-tenant white-label solutions

#### **Technology Approach**
- Cloud-native architecture (vs. legacy Class 5 switches)
- SaaS model for predictable economics
- API-driven platform for integration flexibility
- Multi-device support (desktop, mobile, hard phones)

#### **Competitive Differentiation**
- Deep experience migrating service providers from legacy systems
- Focus on service provider success (not direct-to-consumer)
- Platform agility enabling same-day deployment
- Comprehensive feature set (voice, UCaaS, trunking, PBX)
